Wildfire Mitigation Report

Home Hardening

Roof, Gutter, Eaves
Roofs are prone to ember accumulation and eventual ignition if not fire-resistant. This is especially true for homes with complex roof shapes.
Roof debris is regularly cleaned and maintained.
Openings in tile or metal roofs are filled, all roof patches are in good condition. There are no lifting or missing shingles.
A "Class A" roof is installed, and it is regularly cleared of debris.
Construction
Dry debris can be ignited by wind-blown embers that are traveling from your chimney.
Debris surrounding the chimney is regularly cleared.
The fireplace with stove chimney and flue has the appropriate spark arrestor, which is a minimum of 12-guage welded wire or woven wire mesh with openings that are not exceeding 1/2".
The chimney and stovepipe are coated with non-flammable screening no larger than 1/8" mesh and all debris is removed from the surrounding area.
Structures
Embers can fall between deck boards and ignite the joists beneath or combustibles in the underdeck area and quickly spread to your home.
The deck is isolated from fire with non-combustible materials, such as stone, concrete or gravel along the front, sides, and below the deck creating a fire barrier.
Open space beneath a deck is covered by a skirt of 1/8" or finer non-combustible mesh, exterior fire-retardant treated wood, or other ignition resistant materials.
The deck is covered in a flame-resistant coating or made of non-combustible materials (including joists, railings, posts, and walking surface), has a skirt covering the sides and is cleared of debris.
Windows & Doors
Windows are vulnerable to flame contact and radiation and can explode if exposed to heat for a long period of time. The amount of time you have from the point of heat accumulating and the point of combustion depends on how thick your glass is.
The space below bay windows are enclosed with non-combustible materials to reduce the risk of embers and heat being trapped beneath and the window screens on standard windows are a non-combustible mesh not exceeding 1/8".
Dual or triple pane windows are installed and/or the number and size of windows that face large areas of vegetation are limited. Window screens are made from non-combustible mesh and do not exceed 1/8".
Exterior windows, windows within exterior doors and skylights are tempered glass, multilayered glazed panels, glass block or have a fire-resistant rating of no less than 20 minutes. Window screens are made from non-combustible mesh and do not exceed 1/8".
Fencing
Combustible fences provide a path of fuel for fire to reach a home. Even for homes with non-combustible cladding, flames from a burning fence can threaten eaves and nearby windows.
The fence is coated in a flame-resistant coating, and the maintenance is maintained.
A non-combustible fence or fence transition is installed within 5' of the home (including replacing combustible gates).
Open fencing or gates constructed from non-combustible materials are instead of solid privacy panels to allow embers to pass through rather than accumulate.
Address Plate
In an emergency situation, first responders and citizens reporting a fire need to be able to quickly identify the address of a burning structure. Some homes cannot be seen from the road so they may not know where to go.
The address numbers are displayed in contrasting colors (4" minimum in size) and are accessible from the street or access road.
Additional signage is used to identify large parcels from multiple directions in the event the main road is impassible.
The address numbers are displayed in contrasting colors (4" minimum in size) and are accessible from the street or access road and additional signage is used to identify large parcels from multiple directions in the event the main road is impassible.

Ember Zone

Plants
The first 5' from your home is the most important and the most vulnerable to embers. Angled exterior walls, also known as re-entrant corners, should be given additional space to prevent extreme heat buildup.
Hazardous vegetation is replaced with less flammable plants. An irrigated, well-maintained flower bed is more effective than a dense collection of flammable shrubs.
The amount of vegetation around the home is reduced by the increase of space between the vegetation with non-combustible materials or having placed the plants into individual non-combustible pots/planters.
All vegetation and items that could ignite are removed from the area around the home as well as under decks, windows, balconies and stairs.
Trees
Trees can ignite neighboring trees if planted too closely and tree-to-tree ignitions can create a pathway for wildfire to reach the structure even if mitigation actions are taken to mitigate ground spread. Windy areas and structures located on a slope may require greater distances.
Trees are spaced at least 10' apart to prevent fire spread.
Trees are spaced at least 20' apart to prevent fire spread.
Trees are spaced at least 30' apart to prevent fire spread.
Fuel
Any additional source of fuel surrounding your home should be reduced to prevent ambient heat and flame spread.
All exposed liquid propane or gas tanks, combustible structures, and wood piles have a minimum of 10' of clearance, down to bare mineral soil.
All liquid propane or gas tanks, combustible structures, and wood piles have a minimum of 10' of clearance and are protected by an Approved Fire Blanket or stored in a non-combustible container.
All liquid propane or gas tanks, combustible structures, and wood piles are relocated to a distance of 30' from all structures and have a minimum of 10' of clearance, down to bare mineral soil.
Surface Litter
Embers that land onto surface litter can ignite and create a pathway for the fire to your home.
Surface litter does not exceed a depth of 3" (fallen leaves or needles, twigs, bark, cones, and small branches).
Pathways constructed with non-combustible materials are designed to keep surface litter from the most vulnerable areas of your home.
Annual grass is cut or mowed down to a maximum height of 4" and the area surrounding the home is clear of all surface litter with non-combustible pathways.
Vehicles
Vehicles, Motorhomes, or boats with gasoline engines create risk of gas explosion when ground fire spreads underneath the vehicle.
If the vehicle is operational, any debris or vegetation is removed from the area surrounding and beneath vehicle or parked non-combustible materials such as rocks or stone on top of vegetation to act as a barrier.
If the vehicle is inoperable, the gasoline is drained and any debris or vegetation is removed from the area or parked on non-combustible materials such as rocks or stone, to act as a barrier.
Regardless of whether the vehicle is operational, it is parked at least 30' away from the home and the surrounding area, including underneath, is clear of vegetation.
